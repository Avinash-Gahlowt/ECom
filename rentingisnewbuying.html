Technology is indeed a boon when the demand is met with affordability at a premium choice . This perspective was applied to consumer behaviour which is turn has led to the revolutionary rise of the rental economy which is disrupting the landscape . Renting is easy and savvy, and let's not forget sustainability !

This new way of consuming - call it the Netflix economy gives you the privilege to rent instead of buying: a power drill, a song, a tent, an office for an hour, a Prada handbag, a wedding dress, a painting, a dog, your neighbour's car, a drone.

In 2015 , Rent an Attire was born out of the same personal need clubbed with identifying the business potential for this model . Making designer occasion wear garments available without paying a fortune was the mantra for success.

So could the answer be to forgo ownership of everything else?

In many ways, renting is just a continuation of what people have always done - borrowing a cup of sugar or a pick-up truck from a neighbour. The difference is that technology has made borrowing possible at a broader scale, and between strangers. Social networking profiles and rating systems offer a level of trust and verification, and mobile phones equipped with GPS take much of the work out of pairing people.

There may not be a direct answer to the above question but with renting we have a wider choice to have a mixed bag of fixed and fluid assets and services. The power to not own is a freedom we can exercise in today's dynamic world . So let's explore this many dimensions of this subject in the upcoming blogs.

On a lighter note , in the future; consumers will look to rent a spaceship for a vacation on Mars planet, or simply a group or friends to go clubbing with. Your comments will help us discover many more aspects of this economy .

So pour your thoughts and let's get started .
